The seven wonders of the world or the Seven Wonders of the World (simply known as Seven Wonders) is a list of remarkable constructions of classical antiquity given by various authors in guidebooks or poems popular among ancient Hellenic tourists. Although the list, in its current from, did not stabilize until the Renaissance, the first such lists of seven wonders date from the 2nd - 1st century BC. The original list inspired innumerable versions through the ages, often listing seven entries of the original seven wonders of the world.

Consequently, the Chauhan became the lord of Delhi and Ajmer. Vaisala Deva Chauhan was the great lover of literature, but Prithviraj Chauhan(1168-1192) was the great ruler of this dynasty. Prithviraj Chauhan was a valiant warrior, Chand Bardai, his court poet described the achievements of Prithviraj Chauhan in his famous book 'Prithvirajrasa'.
